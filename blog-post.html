A crowded ferry ride away from Tanzania's coastal city, Dar es Salaam, Zanzibar is a marvel for the senses. Every sight and smell is provocative, inspiring a sense of the old and new.

Zanzibar

The Spice Island

Zanzibar holds so many special memories for me. My husband and I got married there almost 20 years ago. That was the first time that we travelled overseas together. While we were there we did our PADI Open Water Diving Course & dived with sea turtles!

The island is characterized by the magic of historic Stone Town and beautiful sandy beaches fringed with coral reefs.

The Spice Tour is one of the most popular excursions in Zanzibar, which is often referred to as "The Spice Island". Zanzibar is one of the world’s leading producers of spices such as clove, nutmeg and cinnamon. A typical spice tour takes you to an organic farm with a wide variety of plants around the year. You don’t just see the spices & fruits, you also touch, smell and taste.

My favourite experience by far was the diving. Zanzibar is a dream destination for scuba divers, boasting some of the best dives sites in Africa & some say, the world. With average water temperatures of 27 degrees Celsius and visibility between 20 and 60 metres, it is a great diving destination throughout the year.
